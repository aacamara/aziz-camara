The Sign Reversal

Individual Gains ≠ Team-Level Gains

Team SizeCFR βpDirection
Small (1–16)−0.0010.891▼ improving
Medium-small (17–33)−0.0190.068▼ improving
Medium-large (34–57)+0.0180.054▲ degrading
Large (58–496)+0.0220.113▲ degrading

Simpson’s Paradox. Smaller teams see marginal quality improvement. Larger teams see degradation. Anthropic measures individual success (+10%). At the organizational level, the composition shifts. Both findings are correct — at different zoom levels.

November 30, 2022

The ChatGPT Shock

Regression discontinuity around the ChatGPT launch date. What happened in 500 repositories overnight:

−9.5%
Throughput drop · p < 0.001
−16.1%
Lead time drop · p = 0.001
+0.5%
CFR increase · p = 0.073

The J-curve of technology adoption. Output dipped before it rose. Anthropic’s learning curve explains the recovery — but the initial disruption isn’t visible in their cross-sectional data.

The Bigger Picture

The Productivity Paradox Returns

“You can see the computer age everywhere but in the productivity statistics.”
— Erik Brynjolfsson, 1993

Replace “computer” with “AI coding tools.” Thirty years later, the same pattern holds.

Anthropic’s learning curves show individuals getting better at using AI. My data shows organizations absorbing more volume without proportional quality improvement. The real learning curve isn’t just about prompting. It’s about organizational adaptation.
